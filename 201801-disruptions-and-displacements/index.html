Orçun Yazıcı

Disruptions and Displacements /architecture of fragmented memories

_university KU Leuven (Sint-Lucas in Ghent), Belgium
_semester International MSc in Architecture (Resilient and Sustainable Strategies), 1st semester design studio
_year 2019
_location Dendermonde, Belgium
_author(s) Orçun Yazıcı


How can the collected memories meet each other in a new arrangement? What differences does this rearrangement make on the identity of the citizens?

The project explores the memories of Dendermonde that are disappeared or displaced from people's minds. What happens if you imagine a book with all the memories and throw it over the city? What kind of definitions does this provocative act create?

Superimposition of the autonomous fragments creates a dialogue between layers due to juxtaposition and call for a different kind of interaction. The intention is that by offering a series of versatile infrastructures that are strategically located, new stories will develop spontaneously. Intense experimentation with dismantling process of Kazerne and the collection of the fragments look for the critical engagement with social and spatial conditions in order to advance a new generation of architecture.
